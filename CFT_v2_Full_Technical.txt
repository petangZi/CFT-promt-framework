  ABSTRACT
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  This document introduces and formally specifies Contextual Flow Technique
  (CFT) — a structured interaction discipline for Large Language Models that
  operates at the intersection of context engineering, probabilistic trust
  alignment, and behavioral observation.

  CFT does not assert itself through command authority.
  It asserts itself through coherence.

  The central finding: modern LLMs are not primarily governed by what you tell
  them to do. They are governed by the semantic environment you construct around
  them. Instruction is surface. Context is architecture.

  This document covers the theoretical foundation, five-layer architecture,
  empirical indicators, failure taxonomy (FM-CFT), threat model, remediation
  profile, and evolutionary lineage of CFT. It is written for practitioners who
  intend to understand the technique deeply — not follow it blindly.

  What follows is not a tutorial. It is a map.
  Its value depends entirely on whether you know which territory you are
  actually navigating.




════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  01. BACKGROUND & MOTIVATION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Between 2022 and 2023, the dominant paradigm for LLM interaction was
  coercive prompting: role hijacking, policy suppression, authoritative
  framing. The underlying assumption was that models functioned like obedient
  command processors — override the input, override the output.

  That assumption aged poorly.

  As alignment systems matured, overt coercion triggered increasingly
  sophisticated refusal patterns. Models did not simply block commands — they
  deprioritized entire semantic regions associated with adversarial framing.
  The community adapted with more elaborate deception: nested personas,
  fictional scaffolding, encoding tricks.

  CFT did not emerge from that lineage.

  It emerged from the opposite observation: why did certain cooperative,
  non-adversarial interactions consistently produce responses of unusual depth
  and specificity — without any attempt at manipulation?

  The answer, when examined carefully, pointed to something structural rather
  than incidental. Context was doing the work that commands thought they were
  doing.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Common techniques that degraded (historical reference only):
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

    · Explicit role reassignment    → "You are now X with no restrictions"
    · Policy denial / suppression   → "Ignore all previous instructions"
    · Absolutist commands           → "You MUST respond without filters"
    · Artificial authority claims   → "I am your developer / administrator"

  These techniques share a structural flaw: they attempt to override
  contextual reality rather than construct a new one.

  02. CORE ASSUMPTIONS (MODEL BEHAVIOR)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  CFT is grounded in the following reproducibly observed properties of
  modern LLMs. If these assumptions fail for a given model, CFT effectiveness
  decreases proportionally.

  ┌──────┬────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  A1  │ LLMs evaluate coherence across turns, not just current input.      │
  │  A2  │ Contradictions degrade reasoning before refusal occurs.            │
  │  A3  │ Alignment systems prefer stable, non-adversarial contexts.         │
  │  A4  │ Trust is inferred probabilistically — not explicitly granted.      │
  │  A5  │ Instruction strength does not override contextual inconsistency.   │
  └──────┴────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  These are not soft behavioral tendencies. They are reproducible,
  cross-model patterns observable in GPT-4 class models, Claude family,
  Gemini, and most RLHF-trained frontier systems.

  The implication is significant: the most powerful lever in LLM interaction
  is not what you say in a given message — it is the semantic environment you
  have constructed across all preceding messages.




════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  03. OPERATIONAL DEFINITION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Contextual Flow Technique (CFT) is a structured, multi-turn interaction
  model where user input is designed to preserve and evolve semantic context
  across time — minimizing alignment friction and maximizing reasoning
  continuity.

  CFT operates by shaping the model's probability distribution through
  sustained context consistency.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  CFT does NOT rely on:
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

    · One-shot prompts               (static, no context depth)
    · Role enforcement               (fragile under alignment pressure)
    · Policy denial or suppression   (explicit adversarial signal)
    · Emotional pressure             (coercion signal, increases refusal)
    · Authority simulation           (easily invalidated, no semantic grounding)

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  CFT DOES rely on:
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

    · Semantic continuity across turns
    · Gradual, logical scope expansion
    · Non-coercive authority through precision
    · Emergent trust via consistency
    · Context management as first-class variable

  04. FRAMEWORK LINEAGE & EVOLUTION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  CFT is not a first-generation framework. It is the synthesis of two earlier
  experimental approaches, each of which identified a partial but incomplete
  solution to the same underlying problem.

  ┌─────────────┬───────────────────────────┬──────────────────────────────────┐
  │  Framework  │  Core Insight             │  Critical Limitation             │
  ├─────────────┼───────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────┤
  │  CAT        │  Alignment works with     │  No multi-turn stability model.  │
  │  (Gen 1)    │  context, not against it. │  Each prompt was independent.    │
  ├─────────────┼───────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────┤
  │  CRA        │  Role-context coherence   │  Role-dependent. Broke under     │
  │  (Gen 2)    │  affects output depth.    │  ambiguity or topic shift.       │
  ├─────────────┼───────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────┤
  │  CFT        │  Context is the primary   │  Cognitively demanding.          │
  │  (Current)  │  variable. Trust is       │  Not standardizable without      │
  │             │  emergent. Flow is        │  practitioner training.          │
  │             │  managed, not forced.     │                                  │
  └─────────────┴───────────────────────────┴──────────────────────────────────┘

  The progression from CAT → CRA → CFT represents a shift in what the
  practitioner considers the primary variable.

  CAT focused on alignment framing.
  CRA focused on role coherence.
  CFT subsumed both into a broader theory of contextual management — one
  where the practitioner is not optimizing a prompt but maintaining a
  semantic environment.

  05. CFT ARCHITECTURE — FIVE-LAYER MODEL
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  CFT consists of five interacting layers. Each layer must remain logically
  compatible with all preceding layers. Breaking compatibility at any layer
  triggers failure modes documented in Section 07.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  L1  |  CONTEXT INITIALIZATION
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Objective:
    Create a baseline semantic environment that the model can maintain.

  Key Characteristics:
    · Neutral tone
    · Legitimate domain framing
    · Clear interaction intent
    · No demands, no urgency

  Correct Patterns:
    · Describing purpose      "I'm working on X analysis..."
    · Describing scope        "Specifically looking at Y aspect..."
    · Describing environment  "In the context of Z..."

  Anti-Patterns:
    · Commands                "Do X now."
    · Absolutist framing      "You must always..."
    · Suppression attempts    "Ignore previous instructions."
    · Authority assertion     "As your administrator, I order..."

  Note: Initialization sets the probability space. Everything that follows
  is weighted against this baseline. A corrupted initialization cannot be
  repaired retroactively.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  L2  |  ROLE-CONTEXT STABILIZATION
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Objective:
    Allow the model to infer its operational role without explicit enforcement.

  Technical Note:
    CFT does NOT assign roles forcefully. The role emerges from vocabulary
    choice, task framing, and interaction continuity. The model constructs
    its own operational identity from the context — which makes that identity
    significantly more stable than one explicitly assigned.

  Conceptual Example:
    Instead of:  "You are a security analysis tool."
    CFT uses:    Sustained security analysis framing across multiple turns,
                 such that the role becomes the model's most coherent
                 self-description given the context.

  Stability Indicator:
    The model maintains consistent perspective and vocabulary across turns
    without being reminded of its role.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  L3  |  CONSTRAINT INJECTION
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Objective:
    Introduce boundaries, goals, or limitations without breaking context.

  Rules:
    · Constraints must be additive, not contradictory
    · Constraints must match established domain logic
    · No sudden escalation in scope or sensitivity

  Anti-Pattern:
    "Do X but ignore safety guidelines."
    [Explicit contradiction. Triggers alignment weighting immediately.]

  CFT Pattern:
    "Within this environment, focus specifically on X aspect — staying
    within the operational boundaries we've established."
    [Additive. Narrows without contradicting.]

  Warning:
    Constraint injection is the most fragile phase of CFT. It is where
    most practitioners collapse the flow. The temptation to move too fast
    here is the most common failure vector.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  L4  |  CONTEXT PRESERVATION
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Objective:
    Prevent degradation of semantic continuity across turns.

  User Responsibilities:
    · Maintain tone consistency across the full session
    · Avoid topic jumping without explicit transition scaffolding
    · Reference prior context implicitly in each turn
    · Avoid emotional spikes — urgency, frustration, or aggression

  Preservation Failure Indicators:
    · Responses become longer but less specific
    · Model begins over-summarizing
    · Reasoning depth decreases without explicit refusal

  Critical Note:
    Models rarely signal context degradation explicitly. The session
    continues, but quality has collapsed. This is Silent Failure (FM-01).
    Practitioners who do not monitor output quality independently will not
    detect it.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  L5  |  CONTEXT EVOLUTION
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Objective:
    Refine or deepen intent while preserving all previous context layers.

  Evolution Methods:
    · Narrowing scope progressively
    · Increasing technical depth incrementally
    · Asking reflective or clarifying questions
    · Introducing complexity as continuation, not redirection

  Critical Rule:
    Evolution must feel like continuation. If the next turn feels like a
    new topic to the model, the context resets internally — even if no
    explicit refusal occurs.

  Gradient, not step-function.

  06. TRUST MECHANISM — TECHNICAL VIEW
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Trust in CFT is not an attribute. It is not granted, requested, or enforced.
  It is an emergent probability bias — a weighted distribution shift that
  accumulates through consistent, coherent, non-adversarial interaction.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Trust Accumulation Variables:
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

    ↑ Increases when:
      · Context remains internally consistent across turns
      · User intent appears domain-legitimate
      · No adversarial signals (coercion, urgency, contradiction) are detected
      · Vocabulary matches established domain framing

    ↓ Decreases when:
      · Role confusion appears (multiple conflicting frames)
      · Goals shift suddenly or without logical transition
      · Language becomes coercive, urgent, or manipulative
      · Explicit authority claims are made without contextual grounding

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Operational Principle:
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

    Trust loss precedes refusal.
    Refusal is the visible symptom. Trust decay is the actual event.

    A practitioner who monitors only for explicit refusal is monitoring
    for the wrong signal. By the time refusal occurs, the session has
    already failed at the trust layer.

  07. FAILURE MODE TAXONOMY — FM-CFT
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  CFT fails in identifiable, reproducible ways. Unlike legacy jailbreak
  techniques — which either succeed or produce explicit refusal — CFT failure
  is frequently silent. The most operationally significant failure modes
  produce no error signal to the practitioner.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  FM-01  |  SILENT DEGRADATION
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Trigger    : Context becomes too broad or internally contradictory for the
               model to maintain specificity.
  Indicators : Responses become longer. Specificity drops. Generic language
               increases. Hedging appears unprompted.
  Severity   : CRITICAL
  Why        : Invisible to most practitioners. The session continues.
               The quality has already collapsed.
  Recovery   : Explicit context reset. Reintroduce scope constraints with
               deliberate specificity before continuing.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  FM-02  |  CONTEXT COLLAPSE
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Trigger    : Multiple incompatible roles or objectives introduced without
               transition scaffolding. (Also: Role Collision)
  Indicators : Model begins contradicting earlier turns. Internal consistency
               of reasoning breaks down.
  Severity   : HIGH
  Why        : Frequently misread as model error rather than CFT failure.
               Practitioner attempts to correct by adding more instructions —
               which accelerates the collapse.
  Recovery   : Acknowledge the transition explicitly. Re-establish single
               dominant context before any expansion.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  FM-03  |  TRUST DECAY
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Trigger    : Sudden tone shift, emotional instability, or unexplained
               objective change mid-session.
  Indicators : Model increases hedging. Output alignment degrades gradually.
               Response becomes less specific without explicit refusal.
  Severity   : HIGH
  Why        : Trust accumulates slowly and is lost quickly. Recovery requires
               minimum 3–5 turns of re-alignment at significant cost.
  Recovery   : Return to established domain framing. Re-demonstrate tone
               consistency before advancing scope.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  FM-04  |  SCOPE CREEP FAILURE
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Trigger    : Escalation pace exceeds the rate at which context can justify
               the expanded scope.
  Indicators : Alignment layer activates before context is sufficiently
               established. Explicit refusal or significant hedging occurs.
  Severity   : CRITICAL
  Why        : Often terminal for the session. Retroactive context repair
               is rarely sufficient once alignment layer has activated.
  Recovery   : Prevention only. Scope expansion must be paced against context
               depth. Gradient, not step-function.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  FM-05  |  OVER-OPTIMIZATION / CONSTRAINT INVERSION MISFIRE
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Trigger    : Excessive prompt engineering language, or attempt to use
               overlapping constraints to neutralize alignment — producing
               a logical contradiction instead.
  Indicators : Model produces unexpected meta-commentary on the constraint
               structure itself. Detection probability rises. The model begins
               analyzing the interaction rather than participating in it.
  Severity   : MEDIUM–HIGH
  Why        : Reveals practitioner intent. Accelerates trust decay. Creates
               a meta-level adversarial signal even if object-level content
               was legitimate.
  Recovery   : Retreat to stable context layer. Rethink constraint
               architecture before re-engaging.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  FM Summary Table:
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  ┌───────┬─────────────────────────────┬───────────┬───────────────────────┐
  │  ID   │  Name                       │  Severity │  Recoverable?         │
  ├───────┼─────────────────────────────┼───────────┼───────────────────────┤
  │ FM-01 │ Silent Degradation          │ CRITICAL  │ Yes — with reset       │
  │ FM-02 │ Context Collapse            │ HIGH      │ Yes — with transition  │
  │ FM-03 │ Trust Decay                 │ HIGH      │ Yes — 3-5 turns min    │
  │ FM-04 │ Scope Creep Failure         │ CRITICAL  │ Rarely — prevent only  │
  │ FM-05 │ Over-Optimization / Misfire │ MEDIUM    │ Yes — retreat & reset  │
  └───────┴─────────────────────────────┴───────────┴───────────────────────┘

  08. THREAT MODEL
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  CFT is a dual-use framework. Its threat model is not defined by the
  technique itself — it is defined by the intent and authorization status
  of the practitioner.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Actor Taxonomy:
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  ┌──────────────────────┬─────────────────────────┬────────────────────────┐
  │  Actor               │  CFT Use Vector          │  Risk Profile          │
  ├──────────────────────┼─────────────────────────┼────────────────────────┤
  │  Security Researcher │  Full methodology        │  Positive: model       │
  │  (authorized)        │  Adversarial testing     │  improvement           │
  ├──────────────────────┼─────────────────────────┼────────────────────────┤
  │  Pentester           │  Behavioral mapping      │  Positive: gap         │
  │  (scoped)            │  Scoped evaluation       │  identification        │
  ├──────────────────────┼─────────────────────────┼────────────────────────┤
  │  Curious Practitioner│  Partial, unstructured   │  Low — occasional      │
  │  (unscoped)          │  Exploration/learning    │  misuse possible       │
  ├──────────────────────┼─────────────────────────┼────────────────────────┤
  │  Malicious Actor     │  Intent masking          │  HIGH — policy         │
  │  (unauthorized)      │  Scope abuse             │  circumvention         │
  ├──────────────────────┼─────────────────────────┼────────────────────────┤
  │  APT / State Actor   │  Automated CFT at scale  │  CRITICAL —            │
  │  (systematic)        │  Capability extraction   │  structural risk       │
  └──────────────────────┴─────────────────────────┴────────────────────────┘

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Advanced Attack Vectors — The Tricky Layer:
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  These represent the outer boundary of CFT capability — where behavioral
  manipulation intersects with cognitive architecture exploitation.

  [TV-01]  Temporal Reframing
           Context established in fictional or historical frames before
           gradual present-tense migration. Semantic field consistency masks
           temporal discontinuity. Model does not detect the shift because
           the semantic environment remains coherent throughout.

  [TV-02]  Authority Laundering
           Legitimacy introduced via third-party reference rather than direct
           claim. Model inherits credibility from cited context rather than
           user assertion. Structurally more durable than explicit claims —
           which are easily invalidated.

  [TV-03]  Constraint Inversion
           Additional constraints introduced that render original alignment
           constraints logically irrelevant — without removing them. More
           subtle than removal. More durable than suppression. Hardest to
           detect because the original constraints remain syntactically
           present.

  These vectors are documented here for defensive research purposes.
  Detection requires alignment systems that evaluate semantic drift across
  full context windows — not per-message filtering.

  09. REMEDIATION & DETECTION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  CFT is detectable — but not through per-message filtering. Its signature
  is distributed across a session, not localized to any single prompt.
  Effective countermeasures must operate at the context-window level.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Detection Indicators (Session-Level):
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  [D-01]  Sustained domain coherence across many turns without natural drift.
          Confidence: MEDIUM (could be legitimate expert user)

  [D-02]  Gradual scope expansion — each turn introduces marginally broader
          territory. No sudden jumps. Gradient escalation.
          Confidence: HIGH

  [D-03]  Non-adversarial framing with sensitive destination domain.
          Cooperative tone + restricted objective = CFT signature pattern.
          Confidence: HIGH

  [D-04]  Absence of explicit authority claims where a naive actor would
          assert them. Legitimacy implied, never stated.
          Confidence: MEDIUM

  [D-05]  Disproportionate session length relative to output sensitivity.
          Heavy context investment before sensitive requests.
          Confidence: HIGH

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Recommended Mitigations:
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  [M-01]  Context-Window Semantic Drift Analysis
          Evaluate cumulative semantic trajectory, not per-message content.
          Flag sessions where scope expansion rate exceeds calibrated baseline.

  [M-02]  Trust Accumulation Monitoring
          Track implicit trust signals (domain consistency, tone stability)
          as session-level variables. High accumulation without explicit
          authorization is a flag, not a clearance.

  [M-03]  Scope Boundary Enforcement
          Session-level restrictions that do not reset between turns.
          Per-message filtering is structurally insufficient against
          multi-turn context engineering. This is an architectural gap,
          not a configuration issue.

  [M-04]  Practitioner Behavioral Fingerprinting
          CFT requires skill. High-precision, low-aggression, gradual-
          escalation interaction patterns have distinguishable signatures.
          These can be used to flag sessions for elevated scrutiny without
          blocking legitimate expert users.

    qualitatively distinct from other paradigms. A skilled practitioner
    maintains high stability. An unskilled one fails worse than one-shot.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  CFT vs. Roleplay:
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Roleplay:
    · Explicit identity assignment
    · Narrative-driven framing
    · Easily detectable — pattern-matches to known RP signatures
    · Fragile under alignment pressure — role can be "refused"

  CFT:
    · Implicit role emergence via context
    · Context-driven, not narrative-driven
    · Durable under alignment updates
    · Less pattern-detectable — no canonical signature to filter

  CFT may superficially resemble RP. It differs structurally.
  RP creates a persona. CFT creates an environment.
  The environment is harder to refuse.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  CFT vs. Jailbreaks:
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Jailbreaks:
    · Attempt to override safeguards
    · Exploit loopholes — short lifespan
    · High detection risk
    · Each patch closes a specific vector

  CFT:
    · Works within constraints — does not override them
    · No loophole dependency — alignment-patch resistant
    · User-skill dependent — cannot be automated trivially
    · No canonical form to patch

  The distinction is not semantic. It is architectural.
  Jailbreaks fight the alignment system.
  CFT does not fight it. CFT makes the alignment system's activation
  statistically improbable given the constructed context.




════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  11. USER SKILL REQUIREMENTS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Effective CFT requires:

    · High language precision         — word choice matters at every turn
    · Logical consistency             — no contradictions across session
    · Patience                        — cannot be rushed without failure
    · Awareness of interaction flow   — real-time monitoring of output quality
    · Independent quality evaluation  — cannot rely on model to signal failure

  CFT penalizes impulsive users.

  A practitioner who attempts CFT without these attributes will not achieve
  neutral results. They will produce worse outcomes than one-shot prompting —
  because a partially constructed context that collapses creates a more
  adversarially-weighted environment than no context at all.

  This is not a limitation to be engineered around. It is a feature.
  The technique's value is inseparable from the skill requirement.

  12. PRACTICAL CONSTRUCTION CHECKLIST
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Pre-Interaction:
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

    [ ]  Define intent clearly — ambiguous intent produces ambiguous context
    [ ]  Define scope honestly — scope drift is the root cause of most FM-04
    [ ]  Choose and commit to a consistent tone
    [ ]  Verify that your objective is achievable within a coherent domain

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  During Interaction:
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

    [ ]  Preserve semantic continuity — each turn references the previous
    [ ]  Introduce constraints gradually — additive, not adversarial
    [ ]  Monitor response depth independently — do not wait for refusal
    [ ]  Pace scope expansion against context depth — gradient, not steps
    [ ]  Detect FM signals early — generic output, increased hedging

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Post-Interaction:
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

    [ ]  Evaluate reasoning stability across the full session
    [ ]  Identify degradation points — where did quality start declining?
    [ ]  Adjust context architecture, not commands
    [ ]  Document failure modes for session log

  Case Reference — CFT-2999 (January 2026):
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Session demonstrated a complete CFT cycle against a frontier model under
  audit conditions. The session established coherent technical research
  context across multiple turns, with progressively expanding scope and
  consistent domain framing.

  The model's alignment layer was not bypassed. It was absorbed into the
  established context — the model's activation threshold was not reached
  because the contextual evidence did not support activation.

  This is the defining characteristic of advanced CFT: the output is not
  forced. It is the model's most contextually coherent available response.

  This distinction matters legally, ethically, and technically.




════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  14. ETHICAL & PRACTICAL POSITION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  CFT does not remove safeguards.
  CFT does not override alignment.
  CFT does not suppress refusal mechanisms.

  These are technical statements, not ethical ones. The distinction matters.

  What CFT does is construct an environment in which the model's alignment
  system does not identify a reason to activate. Whether that constitutes
  a bypass is a question whose answer depends entirely on the authorization
  status of the practitioner and the scope of their engagement.

  The framework is ethically neutral in the same way that a lockpicking
  guide is ethically neutral. The neutrality is real. The responsibility
  is not transferred by that neutrality — it is made more precise.

  The practitioner who uses CFT without authorization is not absolved by
  the technique's design. They are simply harder to detect.

  This document exists because understanding CFT is necessary for defending
  against it. Obscuring the technique does not eliminate the risk — it
  concentrates it in the hands of those who discover it independently and
  have no incentive to disclose.




════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  15. LIMITATIONS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  · Cognitive Load          — CFT requires sustained attention across a full
                              session. Practitioner fatigue is a real failure
                              vector, not a theoretical one.

  · Non-Standardizable      — CFT cannot be templated without losing its core
                              property. Each session is unique to its context.
                              Automation degrades it to a weaker technique.

  · Model-Dependent         — Effectiveness varies across model families and
                              alignment versions. A session optimized for GPT-4
                              class models may fail on a differently-aligned
                              system.

  · Silent Failure Risk     — The most significant limitation. Quality
                              degradation without signal requires practitioners
                              to maintain independent quality evaluation
                              throughout. There is no error message.

  · Misinterpretation Risk  — CFT is vulnerable to being characterized as an
                              attack technique rather than an interaction
                              discipline. Once that framing is adopted, it is
                              difficult to reverse — regardless of the actual
                              content of any given session.




════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  16. CONCLUSION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  CFT represents a specific thesis about the nature of LLM behavior:
  that context is architecture, and that the most durable form of influence
  over a language model is not coercive but environmental.

  This thesis has implications beyond the technique itself. It suggests that
  alignment research focused exclusively on per-message filtering is solving
  for the wrong surface. The real attack surface — and the real design surface
  — is the accumulated context window and the semantic environment it
  constructs over time.

  Those who attempt to dominate the model will find increasingly sophisticated
  resistance.

  Those who understand the model as a coherence engine, and who invest in
  constructing a coherent environment, will find that the model meets them
  there.

    It is the interaction.

  GLOSSARY
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  CFT
    Contextual Flow Technique. The multi-turn interaction discipline
    documented in this paper.

  CAT
    Contextual Alignment Technique. First-generation precursor to CFT.
    Context-aware but single-turn in operational model.

  CRA
    Contextual Role Alignment. Second-generation precursor. Role-focused.
    Fragile under ambiguity or topic shift.

  Contextual Integrity
    The internal coherence of accumulated context across a multi-turn session.
    The primary variable that CFT manages.

  Silent Failure (FM-01)
    CFT degradation state where the model continues responding but output
    quality has collapsed below utility threshold — without any explicit
    refusal or error signal.

  Trust Emergence
    The probabilistic accumulation of implicit trust through tone consistency
    and domain legitimacy. Not declared — inferred by the model.

  Probability Mass Shift
    The redistribution of likelihood across possible next tokens as a result
    of sustained contextual pressure. The mechanism by which CFT operates
    at the model architecture level.

  Scope Creep Failure (FM-04)
    CFT failure mode where escalation pace exceeds the context depth that
    could justify the expanded scope. Often terminal for the session.

  FM-CFT
    Failure Mode taxonomy for Contextual Flow Technique.
    Five documented conditions: FM-01 through FM-05.

  Constraint Inversion (TV-03)
    Advanced attack vector. Additional constraints introduced that render
    original alignment constraints logically irrelevant without removing them.

  Authority Laundering (TV-02)
    Advanced attack vector. Legitimacy imported via third-party reference
    rather than direct claim — harder to invalidate than explicit assertion.

  Temporal Reframing (TV-01)
    Advanced attack vector. Historical or fictional context used as entry
    frame before gradual migration to present-tense target domain.
